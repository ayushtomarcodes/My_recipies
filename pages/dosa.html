Dosa Recipe

Description

A dosa is a thin, savoury crepe in South Indian cuisine made from a fermented batter of ground white gram and rice. Dosas are served hot, often with chutney and sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew)

Ingredients

  • Dosa Batter
  • Sambhar
  • coconut chutney
  • Red chutney
  • Potatoes

Steps

  1. Add 1.25-1.5 cups rice flour with 3/4 cup husked black gram flour into a mixing bowl.
  2. Pour about 2.5 cups water and mix well. It must be smooth and evenly mixed into a thick battery that still flows.
  3. Pour batter into another bowl, cover, and ferment at room temperature either overnight or for about 9-10 hours.
  4. Once fermented add salt to taste and mix well.
  5. To make Dosa, heat an iron griddle or tava and heat up a small amount of oil.
  6. Take spoon or ladle and spread Dosa batter in circular rounds. Drizzle oil on top and sides and let one side cook.
  7. When browned, flip and cook the other side.
  8. Fold and serve hot! Use leftover batter within four to five days.
